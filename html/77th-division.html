THE 77TH

DIVISION

The 77th Division was a well known division of soldiers from World War 1. They were the first National Army division to be sent to France.

Though Cher Ami is a popular myth from the 77th Division, they aren’t known for solely him. They participated in the largest battle in American history.

They are known by various names, such as “Liberty Division”, “New York’s Own”, or “The Last Battalion”. Most names are referring to their New York roots.

The troop is mostly known by the name “Lost Battalion” due to their extreme circumstances. Placed into the deepest parts of enemy territory, they suffered severe enemy fire, starvation, and thirst.

The 77th Division was actually made up of 4 different battalions. Major Whitlessey was one of the most known members and commanders.

Generals and Majors in these Battalions were known to be relentless and brutal. General Evan Johnson made the order of engaging in battle deep into enemy territory with no retreat.
