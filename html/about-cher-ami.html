Cher Ami was a male black check cock homing pigeon in World War 1. Donated by the US Army’s AEF, Cher Ami was most likely hatched March-April 1918. Cher Ami returned to the US as a war hero on April 16, 1919. Unfortunately, on arrival, he passed away June 13, 1919.

Cher Ami was shot at during his final message delivery, causing major and irrepairable injuries. He had lost his right leg and had a deep wound across his chest, and lost the ability to see in one of his eyes.

The contents of the note Cher Ami delivered fully and simply read: “We are along the road parallel to 276.4. Our own artillery is dropping a barrage directly on us. For heaven’s sake, stop it.

Cher Ami survived his wounds and surgeries and was well enough to live on and receive the Croix de Guerre, an award normally rewarded to war heroes (and humans) for any of their outstanding efforts during combat.
